Talking points For the conversation with Aniket
01
130 metres is not a soft benefit
The Swedish allocation system uses relative proximity as the tiebreaker. At 130m from Hägerneholmsskolan, Ivaan will almost certainly get priority over any other applicant. That compounds: if a sibling comes later, syskonförtur applies too. You can choose a different school — but you hold the strongest card for this one.
How allocation works →
02
The school is fine. Not top of its class.
Hägerneholmsskolan sits 8.7 points below the Täby average on meritvärde, but above the national average. SALSA +1.0 means it's adding genuine value. The real concern is class size — 16.4 vs national average 12.1. Reasonable choice. Not the standout school in a high-performing municipality.
See the full data →
03
The IES queue decision is now, not later
IES Täby is bilingual, English-medium, structured, and the go-to for expat families in northern Stockholm. It only starts at Year 4, so Ivaan won't go for years — but the queue is long. Registering now costs nothing and creates a future option. Waiting until Year 2 may mean losing 4 years of queue position.
Register IES queue →
04
For the international vibe we want
Neither Hägerneholm Förskola nor Abakus (current) has a known expat crowd. Futuraskolan International Preschool in Näsby Park genuinely does — bilingual, IEYC curriculum, families and staff from across the world. It's 5–10 min from 11B. If community matters, this is the move.
