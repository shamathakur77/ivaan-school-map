130 metres is not a soft benefit
The Swedish allocation system uses relative proximity as the tiebreaker. At 130m from Hägerneholmsskolan, Ivaan will almost certainly get priority over any other applicant. That's a structural advantage that compounds: if a sibling comes later, syskonförtur applies too. You can choose a different school — but you hold the best card for this one.
Proximity = priority
The school is fine, not top of its class
Hägerneholmsskolan sits 8.7 points below the Täby average on meritvärde, but above the national average. SALSA +1.0 means it's adding genuine value for its students. The concern is class size (16.4 vs national 12.1) — that's real, not noise. It is a reasonable choice. It is not Täby's standout school. That distinction goes to schools with bilingual tracks.

The IES queue decision is now, not later
IES Täby is bilingual, structured, English-medium, and highly regarded by expat families. It only starts at Year 4, so Ivaan won't go there for years. But the queue is long. Registering now costs nothing and creates an option. If you change your mind, you drop out. If you wait until he's in Year 2 and want to move him, you may have lost 4 years of queue position. This is a free insurance policy.

For the international vibe you want
Neither Hägerneholm Förskola nor Abakus (current) is known for an expat crowd. Futuraskolan International Preschool in Näsby Park is. It's bilingual, uses the IEYC curriculum, and genuinely attracts families from across the world. It's 5–10 min from 11B. If this matters — and you said it does — it's worth visiting and joining that queue in parallel.
